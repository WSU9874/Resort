한림 공원

Jeju Hallim Park is a site with a pioneering spirit that was made into a green paradise by Song Bong-gyu, the founder of the park, sowing palm seeds in a 100,000 pyeong wilderness sand field in 1971. Hallim Park, which features nine fantastic themes, has been visited by prominent people from around the world, including former Chinese President Jiang Zemin and former Japanese Prime Minister Nakasone, and is a global tourist attraction for domestic and foreign tourists.

협재굴

Hyeopjaegul Cave is a representative lava tube of Jeju Island along with Golden Cave, Socheongul Cave, Ssangyonggul Cave, and Manjanggul Cave. The cave is about 200m long, 10m wide, and 5m high, and was created when the volcano around Hallasan erupted 2.5 million years ago. It is peculiar that it is a two-dimensional cave that combines the characteristics of a lava cave and a limestone cave, and the area is made of a sand layer mixed with shells. Inside the cave, stalagmites extending from the ceiling and stalagmites, which look like they are rising from the floor, create a mysterious scene, and the stalactites, where stalagmites and stalagmites meet to form a single pillar, are bizarre and beautiful.

능지리 오름

Nejiri Oreum is an oreum that children and the elderly can climb lightly, and is derived from Nejiri, the old name of Sangmyeong-ri. Since the 16th century, the beacon has been installed, and it is sometimes called "Mangoreum". If you climb to the top of Oreum, you can see the whole area of northwest Jeju Island. The road to the observatory is thick with trees, so you can feel the feeling of walking through the forest, and on a clear day, you can see Hallasan Mountain and other northwestern Oreum from the top observatory.
